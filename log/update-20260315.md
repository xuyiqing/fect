## Design Decisions

1. **est.att.avg â†’ est.avg**: The fect output field for aggregate ATT with CI is `est.avg` (from `fect_boot()` at boot.R line 4653), not `est.att.avg`. The old I8 test and new I9 test both referenced the wrong name. Discovered via auditor BLOCK.

2. **r=0 tolerance 1e-2 not 1e-3**: At r=0, notyettreated (via `inter_fe_ub`) and nevertreated (via `inter_fe`) use different solvers with ~0.003-0.007 ATT divergence. This is structural (different estimation samples), not a bug. Increasing N to 500 shrinks the gap to ~0.0006 but it doesn't monotonically vanish.

3. **CV delegation pattern for cfe+nevertreated**: Mirrored the existing gsynth delegation in `fect_cv` (lines 177-191) rather than adding full CFE CV logic to `fect_cv`. This reuses the already-tested CV loop in `fect_nevertreated` (lines 679-879). Builder discovered a 4th call site (`default.R` line ~1963, the `se=FALSE+CV=TRUE` path) that also needed param forwarding â€” this was not in the original spec.

4. **Quarto book "Two Research Settings" framing**: User directed framing as two research settings (synth vs DID) rather than "two estimation regimes". The synth setting uses never-treated controls to learn time effects (gsynth origin); the DID setting generalizes to EM imputation. This bifurcation also affects inference (parametric vs nonparametric bootstrap).

5. **Preserve gsynth narrative**: User explicitly requested keeping the original gsynth chapter narrative and IFE/MC comparison examples. New content (equivalence callout, "When to use" section) was added after the existing intro, not as a replacement.

6. **em is no-op for nevertreated**: Removed `em=FALSE` from all gsynth equivalence statements in the book. Test I10 confirms em has zero effect under nevertreated â€” `fect_nevertreated` doesn't accept em, and the C++ solvers don't take it.

7. **Chapter reorder**: CFE moved right after Fect Main Program (same IFE family), Gsynth moved after Plots (it's a specific research setting, not a core method).
